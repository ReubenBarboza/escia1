8051 Microcontroller vs ATmega328(Arduino uno)

About The 8051 Microcontroller and Arduino

In 1981, Intel introduced an 8-bit microcontroller called the 8051. It was referred as system on a chip because it had 128 bytes of chip data RAM, 4KB of on-chip ROM, two timers, one serial port, and 4 ports (8-bit wide), all on a single chip.[1]

It has been 39 years since Intel introduced this microcontroller and technology has since progressed immensely.For example ,take the Arduino- a project started at the Interaction Design Institute Ivrea (IDII) in Ivrea, Italy and thereafter prepared the first Arduino by 2005.Since then,the Arduino has become the number one choice for electronics makers, especially for developing solutions for the IoT marketplace, which has been predicted to become a $6 trillion market by 2021.[2]

Advantages of Arduino over 8051 Microcontroller

  • Arduino can handle voltage touching 20V whereas 8051 can handle only till 6.6V[3]
  • Arduino has flash memory of 32KB whereas 8051 microcontroller has only 8KB
  • The clock speed of Arduino can be increased to 20Mhz from 16Mhz while 8051 had a fixed clock speed of 12Mhz
  • One important characteristic of the Arduino over 8051 is that its user-friendly and does not require profound knowledge of its internal architecture like we need it in 8051.
  • Arduino has 14 digital I/O pins whereas 8051 has 32 pins making it more bulky and susceptible to damage.[4]

How could 8051 Improve?

  • Converting 8051 from CISC architecture to RISC would make it more cost efficient and have a faster processing power.
  • Having it programmable by high level langauges like python would be a step in the right direction away from hard to read assembly code.
  • Adding more attention towards building development kits and providing support for them would ease the development process tremendously.
